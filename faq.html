Frequently Asked Questions

Classes & Workshops

No prior experience is necessary! Our classes and workshops are designed for all skill levels, including complete beginners. Barbara provides step-by-step guidance and all the necessary tools and materials.

Classes typically run over multiple sessions throughout a semester, allowing for more in-depth learning and multiple projects. Workshops are one-time events, usually lasting 3-4 hours, where you'll complete a specific project in a single session.

All tools and materials are provided! Just bring yourself and your creativity. You may want to bring reading glasses if you use them for close-up work, and you're welcome to bring a snack or beverage.

Class and workshop sizes are kept small to ensure personalized attention. Typically, we have between 6-8 participants, which creates an intimate and supportive learning environment.

Materials & Techniques

Metal clay is a unique crafting medium made of microscopic metal particles mixed with an organic binder and water. It's pliable like clay, allowing for easy designing, molding, stamping, and carving. After drying, it's fired in a kiln where the binder burns away, leaving pure sintered metal.

We primarily work with fine silver clay and bronze clay. Silver clay is 99.9% pure silver after firing, while bronze clay creates beautiful, durable pieces at a lower cost.

Yes! After firing, metal clay becomes solid metal that is just as durable as traditionally crafted metal jewelry. The pieces can be worn regularly and will last for generations with proper care.

No, metal clay is provided by the instructor at market price. The cost varies depending on whether you choose silver or bronze. This is typically the only additional cost beyond the class or workshop fee.

Jewelry Making Parties

Jewelry making parties require a minimum of 6 people, with 6-8 being an ideal number. This ensures everyone gets personalized attention while keeping the atmosphere fun and social.

Parties can be hosted in your home or business. You provide the space and any refreshments you'd like, and Barbara brings all the necessary tools and materials for jewelry making.

Participants typically create a silver pendant or a pair of earrings. Time permitting, guests can make additional pieces, which can be paid for by weight.

Jewelry making parties typically last 2.5 to 3 hours, providing enough time for everyone to complete their projects while enjoying a relaxed, social atmosphere.
